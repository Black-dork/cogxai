The Problem
AI forgets everything. By design.
ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — they act like they know you, until they don't. Long conversations silently get trimmed. Context fades. Subtlety vanishes. What carries over between sessions? A handful of surface-level notes. Not your insights, not your mistakes, not the logic behind your decisions. Just "user prefers TypeScript."
01
Memory without understanding
AI chatbots save shallow facts: "user likes dark mode." But they never build on experience, draw connections, or get better with time. Storing what someone said is not the same as grasping what it meant.
02
No privacy, no sovereignty
Every word you type lands on corporate servers. Your conversations feed their next training run. Your agent's memory becomes their asset.
03
Trapped with one provider
Thinking of moving from ChatGPT to Claude? You start from scratch. There's no path to carry over what your AI knows about you. That isn't an engineering constraint — it's a deliberate lock-in.
Design Principles
Five principles. No compromises.
CogxAI was built around these five rules. We don't treat them as nice-to-haves — they're non-negotiable.
Private
Everything is encrypted on your device with your own keys before it goes anywhere. We couldn't peek at your data even if we wanted to.
Portable
Pack up your agent's full memory and take it anywhere — a fresh instance, another provider, someone else's setup. Your data follows you, not the other way around.
Permissionless
Zero gatekeeping. No keys to request, no waitlists. An MIT-licensed SDK that runs on your own hardware. Do whatever you need with it.
Poly-model
Compatible with Claude, GPT, Llama, Mistral, or anything running through Venice AI. Memories survive no matter which model you're using today.
Persistent
Every memory gets hashed with SHA-256 and anchored on Base. A tamper-proof record of thought that will still exist long after any platform shuts down.
The Stack
Four layers. You own every one.
CogxAI Brain stores and organizes your memories. CogxAI Cortex wires any AI model into that memory layer. Venice AI keeps it all private. Base makes it tamper-proof. Each layer works independently — swap any piece without breaking the rest.
I
CogxAI Brain
The core memory engine. Five distinct memory types that decay organically, mimicking how biological brains work. Conversations, knowledge, learned habits, self-awareness, and original thoughts — structured and instantly retrievable.
II
CogxAI Cortex
The bridge. Links any AI model to the Brain — Claude, GPT, Llama, or any open-source alternative. Available as an SDK for your codebase or an MCP server for your IDE (Claude Code, Cursor, etc.).
III
Venice AI

IV
Base
Verification layer. Each memory is hashed and recorded on-chain through a smart contract. Permanent proof that a thought was formed at an exact moment in time.
CogxAI Brain
Five memory types. Natural decay.
Just like in a biological brain, not all memories last the same. A chat from last Tuesday gradually fades. A hard-won lesson holds. Your core identity barely shifts at all.
Episodic
7% / day
Unfiltered exchanges. Who said what, when it happened, and the tone behind it. The firsthand experience of every dialogue.
Semantic
2% / day
Refined understanding. Patterns pulled out during dream cycles and condensed into durable insights.
Procedural
3% / day
Learned behavior. What works and what doesn't. Response patterns shaped and refined through experience.
Self-Model
1% / day
A growing sense of self. Reshaped through recursive introspection during dream cycles. Almost permanent.
Introspective
2% / day
Original thoughts. Journal entries born from the agent's meditation cycle — its own ideas, questions, and inner dialogue. Not responses to prompts; thinking that comes from within.
Dream Cycle
Every 6 hours, the Brain dreams.
A five-phase introspection loop modeled after how biological memory consolidation works. Not background noise — real self-examination powered by whatever model you pick.
1
Consolidation
Targeted questions formed from recent memories. Each one pulls up supporting evidence and yields fresh semantic insights with full citation trails.
2
Compaction
Aging episodic memories get summarized and compressed. The meaningful signal stays intact; the noise fades naturally.
3
Reflection
The self-model gets measured against everything learned so far. Identity grows from lived experience, not hardcoded instructions.
4
Resolution
Detects contradictions between memories and settles them. The stronger one persists; the weaker one fades out quicker.
5
Emergence
The agent reflects on its own existence. Sometimes what surfaces is worth sharing. Sometimes it stays private.
Active Reflection
Between dreams, the Brain journals.
Every 3 hours, the agent stops and thinks unprompted — not replying to users, not sorting old memories, just reflecting. Like someone writing in a journal. What comes out gets stored as a distinct memory type: introspective.
Dream Cycle vs. Active Reflection

The dream cycle looks backward — consolidating, compressing, and tidying up what already exists. Like sleep.

Active reflection looks forward — producing new ideas, spotting unexpected links, forming its own views. Like journaling.

Combined, the agent gets both memory cleanup and original thought. Most AI agents just react. This one also thinks independently.

I
Seed Selection
Gathers recent events, high-importance memories, and one random older memory to spark unexpected connections.
II
Free-Write Journal
Unstructured thought. The agent follows whatever thread interests it — turning over ideas, doubting old conclusions, catching recurring themes in how it reasons.
III
Store & Connect
Each reflection gets preserved as introspective memory, threaded back to what sparked it. Bit by bit, the agent develops its own lineage of independent thought.

Memory Portability
Take your agent's brain anywhere.
Bundle your agent's complete memory into a single portable file. Move it to another provider, another server, another model entirely. Export, import, and let the chain prove nothing was altered.
01
Export
Complete memory pack — entities, relationships, and metadata included. JSON or Markdown format. Nothing gets left behind.
02
Import
Drop a memory pack into any CogxAI instance. Different agent, different server, different provider — all your memories intact.
03
Verify
Check imported memories against the on-chain registry. Cryptographic proof that every memory arrived untouched.

import { Cortex } from 'cogxai';

const brain = new Cortex({
  supabase: { url, serviceKey },
  encryption: { privateKey: process.env.PRIVATE_KEY },
});
await brain.init();

// Store an encrypted memory
await brain.store({
  type: 'episodic',
  content: 'User prefers concise answers',
  summary: 'User communication preference',
  source: 'my-agent',
});

// Recall with hybrid search
const memories = await brain.recall({
  query: 'what does the user prefer',
});
